Recycling

Recycling is the process of converting unused materials into new usable materials and objects. There are many ideas that can be done at home.


                These are some ideas for recycling unused items

The First Idea is How To Wrap Gifts Using Recycled Old Shirts

Supplies:
  • Fabric (as t-shirt)
  • Scissors
Instructions (The Steps):
  1. Step 1: Start by cutting out a piece of fabric that is large enough to cover your gift at least twice over. Then place the gift in the middle of the fabric and fold the fabric over on each side, like a hot dog, just as you would if you were wrapping a gift with paper.
  2. Step 2: Next, fold in the ends to create crisp corners, as seen in the photo.
  3. Step 3: Then fold each end upward, onto the gift, so that the two ends now touch each other. *If these pieces are too long, cut these down a little bit so they just barely overlap each otter before moving on to the next step.
  4. Step 4: Next, cut a long strip of fabric that is long enough to wrap around the gift and be tied.
  5. Step 5: Then wrap the fabric strip around the package to cover the loose ends of the fabric that you pulled up in step three. Tie tightly underneath.
  6. Step 6: Add greenery or berries and a gift tag, and you are done.
  7. Now you have a uniquely packaged gift that everyone will appreciate.

The Second Idea is How to make a popsicle stick hexagon shelf

Supplies:
  • popsicle sticks (about 100)
  • tacky glue (most other glue would probably work too)
  • Min-Wax Stain in English Chestnut
  • paintbrush
  • picture hanger (totally optional)
  • hexagon shelf template
Instructions (The Steps):
  1. Step 1: Add a drop of glue to both ends of three popsicle sticks and lay them as shown.
  2. Step 2: Layer three more sticks to complete the hexagon.
  3. Step 3: Continue glueing and layering like this until your hexagon shelf is a deep as you'd like it to be. Mine is about 16 sticks deep.
  4. Step 4: Allow the glue to dry. Do your best to keep your three-year-old from using your in-progress shelf as a wooden Bumbo seat for her dolls.
  5. Step 5: Stain your hex shelf, taking care to get stain in between all of the popsicle sticks.
  6. Step 6: If you'd like, glue a picture hanger to your shelf. I skipped this step because I opted to just put two nails in the wall that the shelf could hang on.
  7. Step 7: Find your cutest homeless home decor knick knack and place it lovingly in its new wall home.

The Third Idea is How to make Curtain Pull Backs From Old CD/DVDs

Supplies:
  • CD or DVD (used or new)
  • Chop sticks
  • Ribbon
  • Artificial flowers
  • Hot Glue Gun
Instructions (The Steps):
  1. Step 1: Trace a circle in the center of the CD. The size does not have to be exact. I used a regular size vegetable can for tracing.
  2. Step 2: Using a sharp utility knife cut out the circle. This required going over and over the lines as the CD is fairly thick. It does NOT have to be a smooth even cut.
  3. Step 3: Pop out the center part that you cut with the knife.
  4. Step 4: Toss the small piece.
  5. Step 5: Wrap your CD circle with ribbon overlapping slightly. Glue down on each end.
  6. Step 6: Glue on flowers of choice. Use a chopstick to attach to curtain. A thin dowel painted would also work.
  7. Now you have a beautiful Curtain Pull Backs.

The last Idea can be fun for children How To Make Egg Carton Flowers

Supplies:
  • cardboard egg cartons or an alternative
  • paint
  • glue
  • scissors
  • paint brush
  • canvas or cardstock
Instructions (The Steps):
  1. Step 1: If working with egg carton cut the “cups” – each cavity makes one cup. If working with any other cartoon, like cup holder, cut in the same manner.
    Cut the sides of each pieces, this being the first step into shaping your egg carton flowers.
    Also cut leaves, stems and a short straight strip from the flat top of the egg carton.
  2. Step 2: Shape the sides, make them round. You will need three cups, one with smaller “petals” and two with larger ones for this project, so keep this in mind as you shape your petals.
    If crafting with younger kids, let them just use one or two cups for one flower as this will make it easier for them.
  3. Step 3: Roll the short straight strip into a spiral.
  4. Step 4: Bring out your favorite floral colors and paint all the petals. Don’t forget to also paint the stem and leaves green.
  5. Step 5: Glue the twirled circle into the center of the smaller, shorter flower piece
  6. Step 6: Glue two egg carton cups one on top of the other.
  7. Step 7: Now glue the small part in the center of the two you previously glued together.
  8. Step 8: You can leave the rolled spiral as it is or you can color it too. Either paint it in the color of the flower or in different colors.
  9. Step 9: Glue the flowers on the canvas (painted or white) or on construction paper.
    Now the beautiful flowers is ready
